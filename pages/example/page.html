In this example we will setup the basic formatting for a MLA style paper.
The first thing we will do is setup the general paragraph format. To do this we will modify the “Normal” paragraph style to fit our needs. To modify the style right click it and select modify.
We will change the font to be 12pt Time New Roman.
Next we will click format then select paragraph.
Here we can change the indentation of the first line to be one half inch.
We will also change the after spacing to be zero and the line spacing to be double. Select OK when finished.
Now we will setup the header. Double click in the top margin of the page to access the header.
From the design tab we will go to page number then top of page and select the right aligned page number.
And add our last name to the header.
Next we will setup the first page. On the first page enter your name, your professor’s name, the course, and the due date. We will also enter the title of the paper.
Since this portion of the paper isn’t meant to be indented we will adjust the indentation by first selecting the lines.
Then moving the indentation indicator along the ruler back to the one inch mark.
The title of the paper should be centered so we will do that by selecting the center alignment in the paragraph menu of the home tab.
Finally we can write the rest of our paper. Notice that we don’t need to manually format each paragraph since we setup the paragraph style at the beginning. We could have also adjusted the title paragraph style and used that for our title if we so desired.
